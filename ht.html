You Don't Need Talent to be an Artist


Back in the day, hard work was the golden ticket to success. But now, things have changed. "Anyone", even if they've scribbled just a few lines on a napkin, can strut into a recording studio, throw together some random words, and ride the "plugs" or "connections" wave. It's a bummer, especially when you've got a killer beat and lyrics that hit hard.


We live in a world where knowing the right people often matters more than raw talent or natural gifts. So, do you really need talent to be an artist these days? It's a question worth mulling over.


And here's the twist: once that "song" catches fire, it's game over. No matter how much lyrical garbage it contains, die-hard fans will rally behind it. But does this hurt the quality of music? Many folks think so. They say that the music scene today has lost some of its sparkle. Why? Because almost anyone can become an artist now. It's tough for some to accept that talent alone isn't the only ticket to the artist club anymore.


In the past, artists could make a splash by dropping songs and the occasional music video. But now, with social media ruling the roost, artists need to strut their stuff on platforms like Instagram and TikTok to grab eyeballs.


Remember the saying, "Hard work beats talent"? Well, it still holds true. Even though artists without innate talent now have a foot in the industry door, it's also watered down the music pool. Being an artist today means more than just nailing your craft. You've gotta be a jack-of-all-trades great at your art, a social media whiz, and a smooth communicator. The music landscape keeps evolving, and talent? Well, it's taken a detour.


Sure, the democratization of the industry lets more artists shine, but it also thins out the exceptional talent pool. Social media and digital platforms? They're the artist's trusty sidekicks now.
